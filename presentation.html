The Problem Statement NEXUS

Why Keyword Matching Fails in Hiring

Traditional recruiter filters overlook high-potential candidates by counting keyword matches rather than modeling role compatibility. The challenge requires building a candidate ranker that behaves like an expert hiring manager.

✕ Keyword Stuffing

Profiles containing endless skill tags but lacking career history alignment game search algorithms.

✕ Synthetic Traps

✕ Compute Limits

Ranking 100,000 candidates must occur in under 5 minutes on CPU without using web APIs (OpenAI, etc.).

System Design NEXUS

The 6-Layer Ranking Architecture

NEXUS replaces simple keyword searches with a multi-layered cascading evaluation engine. Each layer scoring is computed locally on CPU with zero network dependencies.

  • Layer 0 — Integrity Shield: Eliminates honeypots and anomalous profiles.
  • Layer 1 — JD Parser: Dynamically interprets min/ideal exp, skills, and constraints.
  • Layer 2 — Career DNA: Checks title trajectory, product-company ratios, and stuffing checks.
  • Layer 3 — Semantic Scorer: Performs 3-tier cascade filtering using custom BM25.
  • Layer 4 — Behavioral Scorer: Combines 23 Redrob interaction and availability signals.
  • Layer 5 — Ensemble Ranker: Fuses layers, calibrates ranking scores, and writes reasoning.

Layer 0 — Protection NEXUS

Integrity Shield: Achieving 0% Honeypot Rate

To protect the ranking shortlist from synthetic, paradoxical, or corrupted profiles, NEXUS executes 6 independent statistical and timeline integrity checks.

1 Temporal Audits

Detects impossible dates, overlapping employments, and role duration discrepancies.

2 Skill-Exp Paradox

Flags profiles listing "expert" proficiency in skills with 0 months of actual career duration.

3 Title-Career Mismatch

Catches candidates with senior tech titles but purely non-tech/administrative career backgrounds.

4 Skills Inflation

Penalizes profiles claiming simultaneous expert proficiency in 10+ disjointed technologies.

5 Statistical Extremes

Flags uniform exam/assessment scores (e.g. 100/100 across all skills) indicative of bot inputs.

6 Disqualification

Profiles above threshold are penalized to near-zero final score. NEXUS achieved 0.00% honeypots in top-100.

Layer 2 — Contextual Fit NEXUS

Career DNA Trajectory Extraction

A candidate's suitability is not the sum of their keyword matches. NEXUS inspects the chronological structure of their history to assess quality and growth.

✔ Product Company Focus

Calculates the proportion of career months spent at product organizations versus services/consulting firms (TCS, Wipro, Infosys, etc.). Product engineers are prioritised per the job description's implicit preference.

✔ Keyword Stuffing Filter

Compares claimed skills against actual career description logs. If a candidate claims a skill but has zero mention of it or associated technologies in their career timeline, their trust score is heavily penalized.

Layer 4 — Candidate Intent NEXUS

Fusing 23 Redrob Behavioral Signals

A perfect-on-paper candidate is of no use to a recruiter if they are unresponsive or inactive. NEXUS groups 23 behavioral indicators into 5 composite indices used as a final score multiplier.

  • Availability Index: Login frequency, profile update age, and active job-seeking status.
  • Engagement Index: Response rate to recruiter inquiries, message reply latency, and platform clicks.
  • Location Fit: Geographic alignment with JD offices (e.g. Noida, Pune, Mumbai) and relocation willingness.
  • Trust Index: Identity verification, portfolio linkages, and phone validation.
  • Capability Index: Coding assessment completion, code quality indices, and test scores.
  • Multiplicative Modeling: Behavioral scores act as a multiplier (0.50x to 1.20x), ensuring disengaged candidates naturally drop in rank.
